Rebooting my personal website

Sun, 10 Feb 2013

So it finally happened. I found the time to reboot jasonsidabras.com and I figured I should take a bit of time and outline what I have done.

The first is the joy of static web-pages. When I started in HTML, JavaScript wasn't what it is now. There were no databases, and I sure didn't know any python. So I started with HTML and made some gaudy Geocities account. It was wonderful, and I think I got a total of 500 views. Or at least that is what my poorly customized counter would say on the bottom.

So when redesigning my personal web-space, I wanted the throwback of simple HTML. But the world has moved so far from that these days. So, to keep it all together I employed a little help from the static web-page generator Hyde. It uses everything that I love about django and puts it into a static hierarchy. And I write all of my blogs in Markdown, maintain changes of my code at GitHub. NOTE: no one should do anything without some kind of backup solution.

These days comment sections, either personally written, part of a WordPress site, or disqus only cause problems. You have to maintain a database, maintain users, and most importantly minimize spam. So like many other web-blogs, I have done away with them. If someone actually reads this and has comments I have an open twitter account and Reddit is a great place to get the world to join or debate your viewpoint.

The background was from Subtle Patterns. And the top is actually the gray-scaled inverse of the bottom. I also want to mention font-awesome. An amazing font created to give perfectly scaled icons for any web-page. All of the icons on this page are actually a font!

I spend a lot of time at Hacker News and I have read about the advantages of clean and clear fonts, adequate white-space, and the lack of using "pure black" for colors. I used the great Google fonts service to host "Roboto Condensed" and "Source Sans Pro". Both fonts seem highly compatible and are used in slightly different ways. Hopefully as I update this blog more, I will find a writing style. Until then, rambling will work.

My current reading list are the books that I am, well, currently reading. I use the Referly service to keep track of click-through and purchases made because of my links. In doing so, I might get a kickback if you purchase something on the end of those links.

I put these links up, because I am enjoying them, and I hope you do too. Same goes for the links of interest. I have started using Mindful Meditation in order to calm stressful days. Human fMRI studies have shown a cumulative overall brain function health benefit when one practices meditation. I invite you to try it yourself. Finally, check out Brawler for some short stories, comics, poetry, and other forms of media from the local Milwaukee area.

Let's end with the logo. I was trying to think of something nerdy but not circuits, or microwaves, or something obvious. I choose the electron shell diagram for silver. It is simple, can look eloquent, and Sidabras means silver in Lithuanian. Perfect fit.
